Chinese 1 Syllabus

Course Description:

 

Chinese is one of earth’s most ancient civilizations and presently includes almost 20 % of the world’s population. After years of isolation, the nation has opened up and is eager to engage the modern world. To this effort, 200 million people study English in China today---a number equal to 2/3 of the population of the United States. You are one of the fortunate few that will be able to communicate with them without using English! Also, China is fast developing its economy and is providing a large number of working opportunities. With a large land like the United States, China is also a wonderful place for travelers. A good mastery of the language will give you those chances!

 

Course Objectives:

 

This class aims to enable students to communicate effectively in Chinese and to understand the Chinese culture within the language. This class will cover five themes, which are greetings, my family, my friends, at school, and leisure time. Under these themes, students will learn how to greet people, number, how to introduce oneself and one’s family, how to tell dates and time, how to talk about one’s hobbies and the way to entertain/receive guests in contemporary Chinese culture setting. Students will also learn about politeness in Chinese culture.  “Pinyin”, which is very important to master Chinese pronunciation, will also be learned. By the end of this school year, you will know how to recognize between 50-150 Chinese characters, but you will be able to speak and understand much more. We will also learn aspects of Chinese culture, geography, and history.

 

Proficiency ---Level 1:  Students use simple sentences and phrases accurately within a range of vocabulary. Students respond to questions and gather information by asking questions.

 

ACFTL standard of Novice-Mid or Novice-Low proficiency level (Evidence shown as strings of sentences within basic topics)

 

Supplies:

 

In addition to pens, pencils, paper, etc, you will be responsible to bring the following supplies to class everyday:

  • 1”, three-ring binder (with lined paper for occasional note taking)
  • White Board Marker.
  • White board eraser/cloth
  • Colored pencils.

Academic Grading:

Class Participation = 35% of grade

The participation grade is based on these rules:

  1. Actively participate in class.
  2. Speak Chinese.
  3. Stay on task.
  4. Be prepared & on time.

           Homework & Preparation = 35% of grade

           Classwork & Presentations = 10% of grade

           Quizzes and Assessments = 20% of grade

Tests are unannounced and cumulative! 

How prepared you are to actively participate in class will determine most of your grade. Indeed, outside class preparation and in class work supports your ability to actively participate in class. Each unit will have clear objectives stated, and will be measured by a rubric that we will discuss at length.                   

Points from assignments, projects, presentations, unannounced tests and quizzes, and class participation will be totaled and a letter grade will be assigned based on the following:

94-100% =  A       84-86%  = B                   74-76%  =  C            64-66% =D                   

90-93%   =  A-      80-83%  = B-                   70-73%  =  C-          60-63% =D-

87-89%   =  B+     77-79%  = C+                 67-69%  =  D+         0-59%  =F                   

 

Class Participation:

Ten points a day will be given for actively participating.  Students will be required to come prepared, speak Chinese, and participate.  Students will lose points if they are not on task, not prepared, or speak English during class.  In case of an unexcused absence, students will only get 5 out of the 10 participation points.  

Cellphone/tablet usage

Sometimes, we will use or can use cellphones and other technology for classroom purposes. This is up to the discretion of the teacher. I will let the class know when electronics are permitted. Otherwise, we follow the school policy on cellphone use. Please refer to the student handbook or the school website about the electronic usage policy if you have questions or for further clarification.

Tips about success Chinese Learning

  1. Come prepared. Bring your handouts, notes, related assignments to class every period. Remember to also bring a pencil or pen.
  2. Be willing to participate and practice. Participation is crucial. Check your ego at the door, don’t worry about making mistakes, and speak Chinese as much as possible. We will be doing lots of performance and group speaking exercises.
  3. Relax! Learning Chinese should be fun and fascinating. Work to stay engaged at all times-even when it seems hard. With this in mind, you will be surprised at how much you will learn and how quickly you will get to the level you want to be.

Late work and Make up Policy:

Assignments are to be turned in at the beginning of class on the day that they are due.  Students will have 2 days (per absence) to make up work missed for an excused absence.  However, because of the nature of a foreign language class, some assignments will be impossible to make up---even if the absence is excused. 

  • Assignments turned in after the deadline,
    • within one week of the due date will be accepted with a 10% deduction (maximum 90%)
    • within two weeks of the due date will be accepted with a 20% deduction (maximum 80%)
    • within three weeks of the due date will be accepted with a 30% deduction (maximum 70%)
  • No works will be accepted after four weeks from the due date (i.e., 0 grade will be assigned.)
  • Assignments turned in on the last day of the quarter will NOT be accepted.
  • Any exceptions to this policy must be arranged beforehand (e.g., with excused absence).
  • Make-up quizzes & assignments must be completed within 1 week upon a student’s return to class.

If you know that you will be missing a class or have missed class with an excused absence, please see me so that I can give any make-up materials. You will have a week to submit the work without incurring the late work penalty.

Citizenship Grading:

We need to be kind and respectful to one another.  We need to accept others and include everyone.  School policy will be followed.  The student’s citizenship grade will be based on the following:

 

H:  The honorable student is not tardy, and is always prepared with their homework, supplies and assignments.   This student is always on task with an attentive attitude towards learning Chinese, and never is disruptive nor disrespectful.  This student speaks Chinese in class, and does not have any missing assignments.  This is an HONORABLE student.

G:  The good student may be tardy once.  S/he is almost always prepared with their homework, supplies and assignments.  This student is almost always on task with an attentive attitude, and rarely is disruptive or disrespectful.  This student usually speaks Chinese in class, and may have a missing assignment.  This is a GOOD student.

N:  The needs improvement student may be tardy three times.  S/he will sometimes be unprepared with their homework, supplies and assignments.  Three or more times they have been tardy, have missing assignments, forget their homework, use their phone, or speak English in class.  They may be disrespectful and/or disruptive.  This student needs to improve their behavior and participate in class.

U:  The unsatisfactory student may be tardy four times or more.  They may frequently be unprepared, rarely on task, and frequently disrespectful and/or disruptive.  They are dishonest, cheat or misbehave for a substitute. Their phone disrupts class and they do not complete assignments.  This student is not complying with school rules and it affects the other students in class.
